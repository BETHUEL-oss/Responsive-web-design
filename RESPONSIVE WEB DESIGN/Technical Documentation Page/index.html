Introduction

Cybersecurity encopasses the practices, technologies and processes designed to protect systems, networks, and data from digital attacks, unauthorized access and damage.

In an era where digital transformation accelerates, the stakes have never been higher: according to CompTIA's 2025 state of cybersecurity report, organizations must balance evolving threats with resource constraints, as cyber incidents cost the global economy trillions annually.

This documentation provides a foundational overview tailored to 2025's landscape where, AI-driven attacks, supply chain vulnerabilities, and quantum risks dominate headlines.

Common Cyber Threats

The threat landscape in 2025 is marked by sophistication and scale, with attackers leveraging AI for automation and nation-state actors targeting critical infranstracture.

Some of the prevalent threats and their mechanisms:

  • Ransomware: Malware that encrypts data and demands payment for decryption
  • DDoS Attacks: Overwhelming networks with traffic to cause downtime
  • Phishing: Deceptive communications tricking users into revealing sensitibe info.
  • Supply Chain: Compromising third-party vendors to infiltrate targets

Mitigation Tip: Regularly updates systems and conduct vulnerability scans using tools like Nessus or OpenVAS

Encryption Fundamentals

Encryption is the cornerstone of data confidentiality, transforming readable data(plaintext) into an unreadable format(ciphertext) using algorithms and keys.

  • Symmetric Encryption: Uses a single key for both encryption and decryption(e.g.,AES-256).Fast but key distribution is risky.
  • Asymmetric Encryption: Employs public-private key pairs(e.gRSA or ECC). Ideal for secure key exchange.
  • Hashing: One-way function like SHA-256 for integrity checks, not reversible
Network Security Best Practices

Securing networks involves layered defenses to monitor, filter, and respond to traffic. CISA recommends a "defense-in-depth" approach for 2025 enviroments.

Core Components

  • Firewalls: Stateful inspection(eg.,pfSense) to block unauthorized access
  • Intrusion Detection/Prevention System(IDS/IPS): Tools like Snort detect anomalies in real-time.
  • VPNS and Zero Trust Architecture: Verify every access request, regardless of origin-critical amid rising remote work
  • Segmentationo: Isolate networks(eg., VLANs)to limit breach spread.
    • Incident Response Procedures

    When a breach occurs, a structured response minimizes damage.Follow the NIST framework: Preparation,Identification,Containment,Eradication,Recovery,Lessons Learned.

    Step-by-Step Guide

    1. Preparation: Develop an IR plan, train teams, and maintain backups(3-2-1 rule:3 copies,2 media types,1 offside).
    3. Identification: Use tools like Wireshark for anomaly detection.
    4. Containment: Isolate affected systems; short-term(e.g.,disconnect)vs.long-term(e.g.,rebuild).
    5. Eradication and Recovery: Remove malware,restore form clean backups.
    6. Post-Incident: Conduct root-cause analysis and update policies.

    In 2025, AI accelerates triage, but human oversight remains essential for complex nation-state incidents.

    Emerging Trends in Cybersecurity

    The field evolves rapidly,driven by technology and geopolitics.Key in 2025 trends include:

    • AI Exploitation and Defense: Attackers use generative AI for phishing;defenders leverage ML for predictive analytics.
    • IoT and Cloud Vulnerabilities: Billions of devices expand attack surfaces;zero-trust models are non-negotiable.
    • Quantum-Resistance Crypto: Transitioning to algorithms like CRYSTALS-Dilithium amid hardware advandes.
    • Regulatory shifts: Enhanced global standards(e.g.,EU's DORA)emphasize supply chain audits
    • People-Centric Security: Training combats insider threats, with"security culture" as a board-level priority.
